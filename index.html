Bridging Law and Economics: Innovating Research, Education, and Policy

The Centre for Law and Economics at CNLU is dedicated to fostering interdisciplinary research, education, and policy advocacy at the intersection of law and economics. By integrating economic reasoning with legal analysis, the Centre addresses contemporary socio-economic-legal challenges and explores their implications across diverse fields, including competition law, environmental law, corporate law, constitutional law, and beyond. We aim to enhance critical thinking, promote awareness, and offer innovative solutions through seminars, workshops, research programs, and academic collaborations. Our initiatives include certificate courses, student internships, essay competitions, and partnerships with leading institutions and professionals. By driving original research and policy development, we strive to illuminate the economic foundations of legal systems and contribute to the creation of impactful laws and policies for a better society.
